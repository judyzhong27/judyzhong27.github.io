Design challenge

How might we increase discoverability of new content for readers and retailer* presence within our app?
*Retailers refer to large to small businesses that pay us to host their flyer on our platforms.
SOLUTION
We redesigned the home screen to encompass a new featured section and revamped category selection. The new home screen promotes more discoverability for the user curated by our in-house team to bring our customers closer to relevant content.

We also reworked a less-cluttered home screen on our mobile app by adopting a flatter UI which will bring our app to modern design standards.
APPROACH
We collaborated with the sales team, product managers, and key Reebee stakeholders to decide on the best way to highlight our retailers in our app and define the blockers for users to get the content they want.

I used this segway as an opportunity to push for a refresh of the Reebee app design, which hadn't been updated in over five years at the time of this project.

A new home screen design

Research Insights

WHAT
The previous home screen design was underwhelming and offered no sense of hierarchy for flyers to our users. Users expect the home screen to be designed for discovering new content, and browsing aimlessly without much cognitive load.

Our stakeholders wanted to transition to be more brand-focused rather than flyer-focused if we wanted to distinguish ourselves from competitors.

In our market research, our previous design lacked the modern styles of newer apps.
HOW
Redesign the app, starting with the home screen, to give a more modern look that reduces distractions for the readers and entices through improved visual and aims to give more guidance to a user's next steps.
WHY
For the business
Increases user engagement and be more competitive

For the user
Delivers the same intuitive navigational patterns,but minimizes interruptions that comes with cluttered UI components

Moving away from skeuomorphism

Removed shelf style
This aimed to give a more scannable, less cluttered, appearance. The bookshelf of Reebee is intended for browsing and reducing noise as much as possible was key.
Changed the grey background to white
White allowed us to introduce more prominent shadows and really highlight the content our users came for: the flyers. The white background accentuated the flyer which is catered for both user and retailer.
Increased flyer size and added shadows
In our user feedback, the flyers were too small to identify anything tangible for users. We increased the size to encourage to dive deeper into flyers by giving them a sneak peek.

Out with the blue, in with the new

Larger titles
As Reebee grew over the years, their app introduced more features. To keep users in the loop on their place in the flow, we said out with the old and in with new, larger, bolded titles at each app screen.
Rounding out the tabs
Reebee is a family-friendly app and we wanted to reflect that through slight UI modifications. We fully rounded out the tab navigation between current and upcoming flyers to achieve that softer look.
Removed blue header
We opted to remove the entirely blue header for a cleaner UI. It did showcase our brand well, so we developed an onboarding process to emphasize our voice
