Empowering 700 ambitious students to Press Play

The creative direction, user interface designs, and visual assets of an annual conference at the University of Waterloo

OVERVIEW

TEDxUW is an annual conference of over 700 attendees hosted at the University of Waterloo. When I was brought on as the 2019 conference's design director, my task was to handle all the branding, website design, and asset creation for the big event.

The objective of this project is to create a unified, memorable, effective outlook on the TEDxUW conference while working within the constraints of TEDx branding guidelines. We aimed to keep our attendees and applicants in the loop about our event from April to the event date on October 5, 2019.

Press Play

The TEDxUW 2019 theme is Press Play. Press Play means to take responsibility and action in order to seize the day! We want to motivate individuals to go out there and chase what they want and work towards something that provides them with a sense of meaning and peace.

For the logo, I wanted people who saw it to understand the connection with Press Play through its imagery. I modelled it after a video game controller while keeping the iconic X prominent but with a twist on its symmetry. Once my team and I brainstormed for weeks on a proper logo, this is the one we ultimately chose and set on creating the assets of a modern, recognizable, creative theme around the controller.

1. Marketing materials

BRANDING GUIDE

To identify a creative direction for all our branding materials and to ensure consistency across the materials our designers were creating, I worked on a branding guide both for our design team and for the TEDxUW executive team for any materials they created on their own.

I wanted the conference's brand to capture the fun and lively atmosphere that the theme suggested. Each year, TEDxUW aims to have a distinct visual theme in accordance with its theme so there was a great opportunity for me to develop this year's visual language and to give the event an evolution from previous years with a more contemporary approach.

PARTNERSHIP PACKAGE

Each year, TEDxUW's design team will create a partnership package for our team to present to potential event supporters. This is the first big branding project our team was tasked with in May. With this project, we outlined the various sections of the package through a editable Google doc with the design team and the sponsorship team to ensure the copy and the sections were placed in a logical order with ample information for our potential sponsors to inquire about.

For the design, I created fluid shape designs on Procreate to add more brand personality into the package to align with our theme, Press Play. I incorporated several icons as supportive visuals to the content-heavy pages, which also helped to break up the information in more digestible pockets.
