PERSONAL PROJECT

Sunday:
Brunch App
Concept

SUNDAY targets the long wait lines during everyone’s favourite Sunday activity - brunch! This was a personal exploratory project on visuals and animation to deliver an engaging user experience that breathes life back into waiting in line.
MY ROLE
Journey mapping
Visual design
Illustration
Motion design
DURATION
April 2020

Opportunity

How might we incorporate visuals to reenvision the experience of waiting in line for brunch?
BACKGROUND
In recent years, brunch has exploded onto the scene with a demographic scene shifting from basic Sunday family meals after church to a luxurious meal to be enjoyed with friends.

Higher end, experimental, edgier food choices has people flocking, creating wait times upwards of 1 hour for these designated “brunch spots”. So what do you do when all you want to do is have some fancy eggs benny and a side of mimosas with your best friends minus the 1.5 hour wait outside?
SOLUTION
I designed an app that targets brunch restaurants and brunch goers to create virtual waitlists to save time for both parties.

User Research & Insights

Customer Journey Map

To understand the perspective and industry around brunch, I consulted a few different sources:
• Conducting interviews with brunch goers
• Reading reviews of brunch restaurants in relation to the overall experience
• Reading news articles and blogs.
• Going out for brunch with friends at various places around different cities
The significant blocker to the brunch experience are the long wait times to get seated, often coupled with busy hosts who aren't monitoring the list closely or taking down names as they come, leaving customers outside to wait unpredictable times.

Combining vast amounts of people, deliciously innovative food items, few restaurants that offer brunch-specific menus, and spaces that are generally smaller for more intimate gatherings, we see more and more customers having to wait 30+ minutes in line for their meals.

Most of these restaurants have also taken it upon themselves to refuse reservations for the sheer volume of people they get on the weekends and operate on a first-come first-serve basis. What's the alternative?

App structure

Information architecture

Home Screen


When all 3 user stories from the architecture diagram open the app, they’re greeted with a list of nearby brunch restaurants.

At the top of the screen there's a food illustration, with tabs “Restaurant” and “History”. I wanted to keep the functionality simple and omitted further navigation.

From here, we can navigate to our history, view the restaurant’s menu by tapping on the card or join their waitlist.

Restaurant History


We also offer an option for users to view the history with the app - that is, the list of restaurants they’ve been on waitlists on previously.

The difference in information on the cards from the main screen is this shows the party size and date+time of their waitlist.

The option to join that restaurant’s waitlist persists.

A future iteration would be a “Favourites” section for frequent brunch goers to curate a list of their preferred brunch spots in addition to history.

Waitlist Status Card


If the user is already on a waitlist, we assume the moment they enter our app - they want to see how much longer until their table is ready.

We placed a different card style at the top of the list of their waitlist status with all the relevant information they need, since it is a time-sensitive activity.

Within the card they can edit their reservation, view the restaurant’s menu, leave the waitlist, add it to their calendar, or share it with friends.

Add to Waitlist and Confirmation


Once the user finds a brunch restaurant, this modal slides up on the same screen, blurring out the background, prompting the information we’ll need to add them to the waitlist.

Restaurant information such as the address, distance from the user, and hours are present at the top. They submit details like the number of people and email to confirm their position on the waitlist.

Menus


As an added bonus, our app also displays the menus of the brunch restaurants, accessible via the home screen on each restaurant’s card. The option to join or leave the restaurant's waitlist is also included.

Notifications


As a finishing touch, I also envisioned the types of notifications that would be associated with this app to keep users updated on the status of their spot in the waitlist.
