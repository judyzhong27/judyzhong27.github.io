CASE STUDY - 2021

The Pregnancy
Centre's
Donation Hub

The Pregnancy Centre is a non-profit organization based in Kitchener-Waterloo, Canada with the mission of supporting women experiencing unplanned pregnancies with classes, resources and community support.

UW Blueprint is a student-run organization that partners with non-profits to make technology accessible and useful for those who create communities and promote public welfare. That is, building out a tech solution with a full product team to create something that helps their organization operate more efficiently.

In January 2021, we began work on an 8 month project for a web portal where donors and The Pregnancy Centre staff could go to arrange donations more efficiently.
TEAM
5 developers
2 product managers
2 product designers
MY ROLE
Journey mapping
Visual design
Product strategy
Interaction
DURATION
January 2021 - August 2021

Design Challenge

How might we improve the donation experience for donors and The Pregnancy Centre staff?

Impact

The second enhancement of the Donation Hub for the Pregnancy Centre was released in August 2021 and is currently used by The Pregnancy Centre staff.
BACKGROUND
In recent years, brunch has exploded onto the scene with a demographic scene shifting from basic Sunday family meals after church to a luxurious meal to be enjoyed with friends.

Higher end, experimental, edgier food choices has people flocking, creating wait times upwards of 1 hour for these designated “brunch spots”. So what do you do when all you want to do is have some fancy eggs benny and a side of mimosas with your best friends minus the 1.5 hour wait outside?
SOLUTION
I designed an app that targets brunch restaurants and brunch goers to create virtual waitlists to save time for both parties.
