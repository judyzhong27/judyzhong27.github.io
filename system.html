Creating the Waffle Design System

Unifying reebee's visual language across web, mobile, and brand platforms

OVERVIEW

With rapid growth in sight, reebee hopes to build out their design team and plans for several new features within the year. To achieve consistency across the platform, I began to advocate for the importance of a design system for our products to stakeholders.

Our strategy was to create a design system for designers and developers to reference the components and design principles to guide aspects of the user experience from the conceptual building blocks of the product, to the details of the UI.

CLIENT

What's the problem?

Currently, reebee's components and stylings across our platforms are inconsistent (buttons, text fields, workflows, titles, fonts). There are few ideologies set in place to guide design decisions for incoming and existing product team members. This prevents scalability of our product and creates more design debt later on.

3

platforms of reebee are available on iOS, Android and the web

Solution

A Design System

As projects grow over time, and people come and go, it becomes increasingly difficult to align on a vision for design. The business values may change, the industry shifts, and collaboration becomes more complicated. Documentation at each step of the design process was essential to cover all our bases.

Challenges

1. Old design â†’ new design

During the time we were developing this design system, one of my other projects involved redesigning the entire app to refresh its outdated UI according to modern standards and pressure from the competition.

2. Getting everyone focused

The company was very business-driven and oftentimes the product team focused on getting new features out in order to meet new business goals and advance the application. Documenting their work wasn't a top priority and most of the team weren't aware of design systems in the first place.

3. Cross-platform uniqueness

Inconsistences between platforms made documentation difficult, and consulting the Material Design, Human Interface Guidelines, and Web Design practices and the specific properties of each one needed to make its way into the design system.

Learning phase

The idea for the project came upon hearing about how each platform of reebee was shockingly different - different components, design, colours, icons, etc. To start, I investigate and took screenshots of the app on iOS and Android and the web, put them side by side to review its uniformity and how to prioritize building the design system.

Figure: Samples of buttons you'll see on reebee's platforms today

Evidently, the buttons are random on different platforms looking irregular with little guidance provided to developers on which standard to use, and how to create that set component in implementation.

reebee valued giving iOS and Android a design that matched their respective operating systems in a way that the users would recognize. However, in development the two platforms diverged more and more the past few years without a redesign and have proceeded to deliver different styles and experiences to users on either platform.

Figure: The home screen and search results screen of Android placed next to iOS and the web

First steps

As I began building the system, I researched extensively on existing design systems such as Shopify's Polaris, Skyscanner's Backpack, and Salesforce's Lightning Design System.

To unify our workflow across the company, I set out to identify guiding principles for reebee. I had only been at the company a short time, and thought to interview our co-founders and employees to get a better impression of what they valued for our product and in company branding, in addition to what our customers responded to.

Figure: Interviewing Tobiasz and Michal, reebee's co-founders

INTRODUCING THE WAFFLE DESIGN SYSTEM

The reebee Waffle Design System includes design guidelines, components and principles of our brand and mission. It is a central location for current and future designers and developers of reebee to refine our products and keep up with a changing industry.

With a library of standards, designers can focus on improving the user experience, interactions, and workflows our users know us for.

Naming explanation: During my co-op term, reebee hosted Waffle Wednesdays with free waffles for the whole office.

Principles





Intuitive by nature


Our users rely on our product to deliver a frictionless, simple application. Straying away from complexity brings users closer to their goals and keeps them informed on next steps.





Content first


The content from our partnerships is our primary selling point to users. The presentation of our flyers encourages discoverability and enhances engagement.





Driven by data


All product decisions come from user feedback and multiple customer reviews.
